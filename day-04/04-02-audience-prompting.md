# Audience Prompting: Tell ChatGPT Who It's For

Same question, different audience, completely different answer. Ask ChatGPT to explain budgeting to a CFO, and you get one version. Ask for a version for a teenager with their first paycheck, and suddenly the robot puts on different shoes. That is audience prompting, and yes, it matters more than most beginners think.

## Why this matters

A lot of bad AI output is not actually "wrong." It is just aimed at the wrong person.

Maybe the answer is too technical, too simple, too stiff, too vague, or full of jargon that sounds like it came from a corporate training VHS. The problem is usually not just the topic. It is that ChatGPT does not know who the answer is for.

Audience prompting fixes that. When you tell ChatGPT the intended reader, listener, or customer, it can adjust vocabulary, examples, depth, and structure.

## The idea in plain English

Audience prompting means you explicitly tell ChatGPT who the content is meant for.

That could be:

- a complete beginner
- a busy manager
- a skeptical customer
- parents
- high school students
- non-technical employees
- small business owners

You are not changing the facts. You are changing how those facts are explained.

A useful audience prompt often includes:

| Part | What to specify |
|---|---|
| Who | The intended audience |
| Knowledge level | Beginner, intermediate, expert |
| Needs | What they care about or struggle with |
| Format | Email, explanation, script, checklist, summary |

## Simple example

Instead of saying:

"Explain phishing scams."

Try:

"Explain phishing scams for older adults who are not tech-savvy. Use simple language, give 3 real-life examples, and include what to do if they click a suspicious link."

See the difference? Now ChatGPT knows:
- who it is speaking to
- how simple to make the language
- what examples to include
- what practical help to give

That is the difference between useful and "technically an answer."

## Quick tips

- Name the audience directly: "for new managers," "for first-time dog owners," "for customers who are frustrated."
- Include their knowledge level.
- Mention what they care about: time, money, simplicity, risk, confidence.
- Ask for examples that would make sense to that audience.
- If the answer misses the mark, say: "Rewrite this for a less technical audience" or "Make this more appropriate for busy parents."

## Quick summary

Audience prompting tells ChatGPT who the answer is for. That helps it choose the right language, examples, depth, and structure. If a response feels off, the content may not be the problem. The audience probably is.
