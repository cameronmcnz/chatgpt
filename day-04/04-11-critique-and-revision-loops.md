# Critique and Revision Loops

Your first prompt is rarely your best prompt, and your first AI answer is rarely the final answer. That is not failure, that is the process. Think of it less like ordering fast food, and more like editing a draft with a very fast assistant who never gets tired.

## Why this matters

Beginners often ask ChatGPT for something once, get a decent answer, and stop there. The result is usually fine, but not great. A critique and revision loop helps you turn "pretty good" into "actually useful."

Instead of asking for one answer and hoping for magic, you ask for an answer, ask for a critique of that answer, then ask for a revision based on the critique. It is simple, practical, and surprisingly effective.

## The idea in plain English

A critique and revision loop is exactly what it sounds like:

1. Ask ChatGPT to create a draft.
2. Ask it to critique the draft.
3. Ask it to revise the draft using that critique.
4. Repeat if needed.

This works because ChatGPT is often better when it gets to evaluate and improve something specific, rather than trying to nail everything in one shot.

A simple loop looks like this:

| Step | What you ask |
|---|---|
| Draft | "Write a short email asking for a deadline extension." |
| Critique | "Critique this email for clarity, tone, and professionalism." |
| Revise | "Now rewrite it based on that critique." |

That is the loop. Nothing fancy, but very effective.

## Simple example

Let’s say you ask:

**Prompt:**  
"Write a friendly but professional LinkedIn post announcing my new consulting business."

Then follow with:

**Critique prompt:**  
"Review your draft. What feels too vague, too generic, or less convincing? List 3 improvements."

Then:

**Revision prompt:**  
"Rewrite the post using those improvements. Keep it warm, specific, and under 120 words."

Now you are not just generating text. You are shaping it.

## Quick tips

- Ask for a **specific critique**, not just "make it better."
- Name the criteria you care about, like clarity, tone, accuracy, persuasion, or length.
- Ask for the critique **before** the rewrite. Otherwise it may quietly change things without telling you why.
- Limit the number of revision goals. If you ask for 19 improvements at once, things get messy fast.
- Use loops for emails, posts, resumes, plans, summaries, and scripts.
- Stop after a couple of rounds if the changes get tiny. At some point, you are just rearranging deck chairs.

## Quick summary

Critique and revision loops are a simple way to get better results from ChatGPT. Start with a draft, ask for a focused critique, then ask for a rewrite based on that feedback. One prompt gets you an answer. A loop gets you a stronger one.
