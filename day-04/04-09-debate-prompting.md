# Debate Prompting: Make ChatGPT Argue Both Sides

If ChatGPT keeps agreeing with your first idea like an overeager intern, debate prompting fixes that fast.

## Why this matters

Beginners often ask ChatGPT one question, get one confident answer, and assume that is the whole picture. That is how you end up making decisions based on the first decent-sounding response.

Debate prompting helps you pressure-test ideas. Instead of asking for a single answer, you ask ChatGPT to present competing viewpoints. It is like getting a mini panel discussion without booking a conference room or waking up cable news.

This is especially useful for decisions, planning, writing, business ideas, and anything where tradeoffs matter.

## The idea in plain English

Debate prompting means asking ChatGPT to argue multiple sides of an issue before giving a conclusion.

You are not asking it to be dramatic. You are asking it to compare perspectives clearly and fairly.

A simple structure looks like this:

1. State the question or decision.
2. Ask for the strongest case for one side.
3. Ask for the strongest case for the other side.
4. Ask for a balanced conclusion or recommendation.

This works because it forces the model to slow down and explore tension, instead of racing to a neat answer.

## Simple example

Let’s say you are deciding whether to work from home full-time.

**Prompt:**

"Debate this decision: Should I work from home full-time?  
Give me:
- the strongest argument in favor
- the strongest argument against
- the main tradeoffs
- a balanced recommendation for someone who values flexibility but also wants career growth"

That prompt is already much better than:

"Should I work from home?"

Here is the difference:

| Weak prompt | Better debate prompt |
|---|---|
| Should I work from home? | Debate whether I should work from home full-time. Give the best case for and against, then summarize the tradeoffs and recommendation for my priorities. |

## Quick tips

- Ask for the **strongest case** on each side, not a weak straw-man version.
- Give your **goal or priority**, so the final recommendation fits your situation.
- Ask for **tradeoffs**, because that is where the useful thinking usually lives.
- If needed, add, "Do not pick a side until both arguments are fully explained."
- Use this for choices, not basic facts. There is no need to debate whether Tuesday comes after Monday.

## Quick summary

Debate prompting tells ChatGPT to explore both sides before landing on an answer. It helps you avoid one-sided responses, see tradeoffs more clearly, and make better decisions. When the question has real pros and cons, ask for a debate, not just a verdict.
