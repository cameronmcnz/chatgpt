# Lens Prompting: Change the View, Change the Answer

Ask the same question two different ways, and ChatGPT can sound like two different people in two different rooms. That is the whole game with lens prompting. You are not changing the topic, you are changing the angle.

## Why this matters

Beginners often ask for an answer, get something bland, and assume ChatGPT is just being... ChatGPT. But usually the problem is not the tool, it is the viewpoint.

Lens prompting helps when you want the model to analyze something from a specific perspective, like cost, risk, customer experience, ethics, simplicity, or long-term impact. It turns a vague answer into a useful one.

Think of it like swapping camera lenses. Same scene, different result.

## The idea in plain English

A *lens* is the perspective you want ChatGPT to use when looking at a problem.

Instead of asking:

- "Should I start a newsletter?"

You ask:

- "Should I start a newsletter? Evaluate it through a marketing lens."
- "Should I start a newsletter? Evaluate it through a time-management lens."
- "Should I start a newsletter? Evaluate it through a financial lens."

Each lens highlights different things. A marketing lens might focus on audience growth. A financial lens might focus on cost and return. A time-management lens might point out that yes, this sounds great until it starts eating your Thursdays.

Lens prompting is especially useful for decisions, planning, writing, business ideas, and problem-solving.

## Simple example

Here is a plain prompt:

**"Should a small business start offering live chat support?"**

Now add lenses:

| Lens | What ChatGPT will likely focus on |
|---|---|
| Customer service lens | Response speed, satisfaction, trust |
| Financial lens | Staffing cost, software cost, ROI |
| Operations lens | Workflow, team capacity, training |
| Brand lens | Tone, customer experience, reputation |

A stronger prompt might be:

**"Evaluate whether a small business should offer live chat support. Analyze it through three lenses: customer experience, operational workload, and cost. Give pros, cons, and a final recommendation."**

Now you are much more likely to get something practical instead of a generic shrug in paragraph form.

## Quick tips

- Name the lens clearly: cost, risk, legal, customer, teacher, manager, beginner, editor, investor.
- Ask for more than one lens when comparing tradeoffs.
- Tell ChatGPT how to organize the answer, like bullets, table, or recommendation.
- Use lens prompting when the first answer feels too broad or too safe.
- Combine it with other techniques later, like role, tone, or constraints, but do not pile on ten things at once like you are programming a VCR in 1992.

## Quick summary

Lens prompting means telling ChatGPT *what perspective to use*. It helps you get sharper, more relevant answers by focusing on the angle that matters most. Same question, different lens, better result.
