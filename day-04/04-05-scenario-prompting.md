# Scenario Prompting: Give ChatGPT a Situation, Not Just a Task

If ChatGPT keeps giving you generic, blah answers, the problem might not be the tool. It might be the setup. Scenario prompting fixes that by giving the AI a specific situation to respond to, like handing an actor a script instead of saying, "Just go be interesting."

## Why this matters

Beginners often ask for something broad, like "write an email" or "help me prepare for a conversation," then get a response that feels stiff, vague, or weirdly corporate.

That happens because the AI is missing the scene.

Scenario prompting gives ChatGPT the context of what is happening, who is involved, what the goal is, and what the tension or stakes are. Once it understands the situation, the response usually gets much more useful, and a lot more human.

## The idea in plain English

Scenario prompting means you describe a realistic situation and ask ChatGPT to respond inside that situation.

You are not just asking for content. You are giving it a setting.

A good scenario often includes:
- who the people are
- what is happening
- what the goal is
- what makes the situation tricky
- what kind of response you want

Think of it like this: instead of saying, "Help me talk to my boss," you say, "I need to talk to my boss about a missed deadline. I want to take responsibility without sounding panicked, and I also want to propose a recovery plan."

Now ChatGPT has something real to work with.

## Simple example

Here is a weak prompt:

"Write me a message to a client."

Here is a scenario prompt:

"I need to send a message to a client whose project will be delayed by three days. I want to sound honest, calm, and professional. I do not want to make excuses. I want to briefly explain the delay, reassure them that the work is on track, and offer a revised delivery date. Write two versions: one formal and one friendly."

See the difference? The second one gives ChatGPT a scene, a goal, and boundaries. That usually leads to a much better result.

## Quick tips

- Include the situation, not just the assignment.
- Mention the goal clearly.
- Add the tension or challenge, if there is one.
- Say what tone you want.
- Ask for multiple versions if you want options.
- If the first answer feels off, adjust the scenario, not just the wording.

## Quick summary

Scenario prompting works by putting ChatGPT inside a specific situation. When you describe what is happening, who is involved, and what outcome you want, the response becomes more relevant, realistic, and useful. Less generic robot memo, more "yes, that actually fits this moment."
