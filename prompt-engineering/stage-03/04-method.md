# Method

Method is the part of a prompt that tells ChatGPT how to do the task, not just what task to do. If the goal is the destination, method is the route. Without it, the AI might still get somewhere useful, but it may take the scenic route, miss a turn, or show up wearing metaphorical flip-flops to a business meeting.

## What this term means

In prompt engineering, **Method** means the **process ChatGPT should follow** to complete your request.

You are not only asking for an outcome. You are giving instructions for the path the model should take to get there. That path might include analyzing first, organizing information, working step by step, comparing options, or transforming messy input into something cleaner and easier to use.

For example, compare these two prompts:

- "Write a summary of this article."
- "Read this article, identify the three main ideas, then write a five-sentence summary in plain English."

Both ask for a summary, but the second one includes a method. It tells the AI how to approach the work.

This matters because ChatGPT is very responsive to process cues. If you specify the process, you usually get output that is more structured, more relevant, and less random.

You may also see this idea described with similar terms like **approach**, **workflow**, **process**, or **steps**. They all point to the same basic concept: tell the AI how to proceed.

## Why it improves a prompt

Adding method improves a prompt because it reduces ambiguity.

A vague prompt leaves too much room for interpretation. That can be fine for brainstorming, but not so great when you want a reliable result. Method helps by giving the AI a sequence or procedure to follow.

Here is what method often improves:

- **Clarity**: The AI knows what order to tackle the task in.
- **Consistency**: Repeated prompts produce more similar results.
- **Control**: You shape the reasoning style without micromanaging every word.
- **Quality**: Breaking a task into steps often leads to stronger output.
- **Usefulness**: The answer is more likely to match how people actually solve the problem.

Method is especially useful for tasks like summarizing, editing, planning, ranking, analyzing, and transforming information.

It also shows up in prompt frameworks. For example:

- **SNAP** uses **Apply** as the method step.
- **SORT** uses **Transform** as the method step.
- **CLAW** uses **Workflow** as the method step.

Different framework, same idea: tell the model what process to follow.

## Similar words you might see

These often mean nearly the same thing in a prompt:

- **Approach**
- **Workflow**
- **Process**
- **Steps**

If you see any of those, you are usually dealing with method.

## 3 sample prompts

### Sample prompt 1

> Review the meeting notes below. First, group the notes into key themes. Next, identify action items and owners. Then create a short summary for executives in bullet points. Keep the tone professional and concise.

This prompt uses **method** by giving ChatGPT a clear sequence: group, identify, then summarize. Instead of just saying "summarize these notes," it explains the workflow.

Why this improves the prompt: the output is more organized and more likely to reflect the actual priorities in the notes. It also reduces the chance that the AI skips action items, which would be a pretty classic "I technically did the task" move.

### Sample prompt 2

> Compare these three software tools for a small business. Use this process: list the core features of each tool, note the pros and cons, rank them by ease of use, then recommend the best option for a team of 10 people with a limited budget.

This prompt uses **method** by defining a step-by-step approach for comparison. The AI is not left guessing how to evaluate the tools.

Why this improves the prompt: it creates a more useful answer because the comparison has structure. Instead of a vague opinion dump, you get features, tradeoffs, ranking, and a recommendation tied to a real scenario.

### Sample prompt 3

> Turn the rough draft below into a polished client email. First, fix grammar and awkward phrasing. Second, remove unnecessary repetition. Third, rewrite the message so it sounds confident and friendly. Finally, provide the final email and a short list of the biggest changes you made.

This prompt uses **method** by laying out editing steps in order. It tells ChatGPT how to transform the draft, not just that it should "make it better."

Why this improves the prompt: editing becomes more intentional. The model cleans the writing, improves tone, and explains the changes. That makes the result easier to trust, and much easier to learn from if you are trying to improve your own writing.

## Quick summary

Method is the recipe in your prompt, and without it, ChatGPT may still cook something edible, but you might not want to serve it to guests.
