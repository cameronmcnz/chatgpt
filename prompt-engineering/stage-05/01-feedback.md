# Feedback

Feedback in prompt engineering is the part where you ask the AI to improve something, not just produce something. It is less "make me a sandwich" and more "taste this, tell me what is off, and fix it." That small shift matters a lot.

## What this term means

**Feedback** means asking for **suggestions for improvement**.

In a prompt, feedback tells the AI to look at an answer, draft, plan, or idea and point out what could be better. It can also ask the AI to recommend changes directly. Instead of stopping at a first attempt, you invite the model to critique, adjust, and strengthen the result.

In practical terms, feedback often sounds like this:

- "Suggest improvements"
- "Tell me what is unclear"
- "Identify weak points"
- "Recommend revisions"
- "Offer fixes"

This is especially useful when your first prompt gets you something that is close, but not quite there. Which, to be fair, is most first drafts. Humans have been doing rough drafts forever. AI did not magically cancel that.

In prompt engineering, feedback is often part of an iterative process. You generate something, review it, ask for feedback, then revise. That loop usually produces better results than hoping your first prompt lands like a perfect movie monologue on take one.

## Why it improves a prompt

Adding feedback to a prompt improves it because it gives the AI a second job: not just creating, but evaluating and improving.

Without feedback, a prompt might produce:

- vague writing
- missing steps
- weak structure
- awkward wording
- unnecessary fluff

With feedback, the AI is more likely to:

- spot problems
- explain what needs work
- suggest specific changes
- strengthen clarity and usefulness
- help you refine the output for your real goal

Feedback is also helpful because it makes your prompt more controlled. Instead of saying, "write this," you can say, "write this, then tell me how to make it better." That gives you a built-in revision process.

This term shows up in several prompt frameworks too. For example:

- **DRIFT** includes **Feedback** as a dedicated step after defining, refining, and iterating.
- **ROAST** uses **Suggest**, which is essentially the feedback step dressed in a different outfit.
- **REFINE** includes **Improve**, another close relative of feedback.

So, feedback is not just a nice extra. In many frameworks, it is one of the core moves that turns a rough prompt into a useful one.

## Similar words you might see

You may also see feedback described with words like:

- **Improve**
- **Suggest**
- **Offer improvements**
- **Remediate**

These are all pointing at the same idea: help make the output better, clearer, or more effective.

## 3 sample prompts

### Sample prompt 1

> Write a 200-word introduction for a blog post about saving money on groceries. Then give feedback on your draft by identifying 3 weak points and suggesting specific improvements for each.

This prompt uses **feedback** by asking the AI not only to write the introduction, but also to review its own work and suggest improvements.

That improves the prompt because it creates an immediate revision step. Instead of accepting the first draft at face value, you get a mini editor built in. This often leads to clearer, stronger writing with less back-and-forth.

### Sample prompt 2

> Review this email I wrote to my manager asking for deadline flexibility. Offer improvements to make it sound professional, confident, and respectful. Then rewrite the email using your suggestions.  
>  
> Email: "Hi, I am behind on this project and need more time. Let me know if that is okay."

This prompt uses feedback by explicitly asking the model to **offer improvements** before rewriting the email.

That improves the prompt because it separates diagnosis from revision. First, the AI identifies what is weak, such as tone, clarity, or wording. Then it applies those suggestions. This gives you both the improved version and the reasoning behind it, which is much more useful than a mystery rewrite from the void.

### Sample prompt 3

> I am preparing a presentation on cybersecurity basics for non-technical employees. Suggest improvements to this outline by pointing out missing topics, confusing sections, and places where the order could be better. Then provide a revised outline.  
>  
> Outline:  
> 1. What is cybersecurity?  
> 2. Passwords  
> 3. Phishing  
> 4. Company policies

This prompt uses feedback by asking the AI to analyze the outline and **suggest improvements** related to completeness, clarity, and structure.

That improves the prompt because the AI has a clear review target. It knows to look for gaps, confusing flow, and missing essentials. The result is usually a more organized and useful presentation outline, which is nice, because confused employees clicking mystery links is how everyone's Tuesday gets ruined.

## Quick summary

Feedback is the prompt engineering habit of asking, "How can this be better?" and that question alone can rescue a shocking number of mediocre outputs from a very average fate.
