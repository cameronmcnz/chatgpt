# FAST

FAST is a beginner-friendly prompt shortcut for getting better answers without writing a novel. It stands for **Format, Audience, Style, Task**.

If your prompts keep getting replies that are too vague, too long, too robotic, or weirdly aimed at the wrong person, FAST fixes a lot of that. It gives ChatGPT four simple instructions: what to make, who it is for, how it should sound, and what job it needs to do.

Think of it like giving directions to a very smart intern. If you only say, "make something useful," you get chaos. If you use FAST, you get something you can actually use.

## What the template means

| Letter | Prompt ingredient | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| F | Format | Tells ChatGPT how to arrange the answer, such as a list, email, table, script, summary, or bullet points. |
| A | Audience | Tells ChatGPT who the answer is for, so it can match the right level, examples, and vocabulary. |
| S | Style | Tells ChatGPT how the response should sound, such as friendly, formal, persuasive, calm, direct, or funny. |
| T | Task | Tells ChatGPT the exact job to do, such as explain, write, summarize, brainstorm, compare, or rewrite. |

## Why this template works

FAST works because it removes guesswork.

A lot of weak prompts fail for very ordinary reasons:
- The model does not know what kind of output you want.
- It does not know who the content is for.
- It does not know how polished, casual, or serious to sound.
- It does not know the exact action you want performed.

So instead of asking, "Help me with marketing," you give four useful signals:
- **Format**: a LinkedIn post
- **Audience**: small business owners
- **Style**: upbeat and practical
- **Task**: write a post about email list building

That tiny bit of structure often turns a mushy answer into a usable one.

FAST is especially good in the **Beginner Foundations** stage because this is where you learn to ask clearly. You are not trying to become a prompt wizard in a neon trench coat. You are learning how to give clear instructions.

## 3 sample prompts

### Sample prompt 1: Formal version

> FORMAT: Write the response as a 5-bullet executive summary.  
> AUDIENCE: Busy department managers with no technical background.  
> STYLE: Clear, professional, and concise.  
> TASK: Explain the main benefits of using AI chat tools for internal knowledge sharing.

**How the template shows up:**
- **F, Format:** "Write the response as a 5-bullet executive summary" defines the output structure.
- **A, Audience:** "Busy department managers with no technical background" tells ChatGPT who will read it.
- **S, Style:** "Clear, professional, and concise" sets the tone.
- **T, Task:** "Explain the main benefits of using AI chat tools for internal knowledge sharing" gives the exact job.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This prompt is strong because every major decision is already made. ChatGPT does not have to guess whether you want a full article, a casual explanation, or technical jargon. The result is more likely to be short, business-friendly, and relevant to non-technical leaders. In other words, less cleanup for you.

### Sample prompt 2: Semi-structured version

> Create a short email for parents about a school schedule change.  
> - Format: 3 short paragraphs  
> - Audience: parents of middle school students  
> - Style: warm, reassuring, and easy to understand  
> - Task: explain that Friday dismissal will be 90 minutes early because of staff training

**How the template shows up:**
- **F, Format:** "3 short paragraphs" controls the structure.
- **A, Audience:** "parents of middle school students" helps the model choose understandable wording and relevant details.
- **S, Style:** "warm, reassuring, and easy to understand" prevents the message from sounding cold or corporate.
- **T, Task:** "explain that Friday dismissal will be 90 minutes early because of staff training" defines the content goal.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
Without FAST, ChatGPT might write something too formal, too long, or too vague. This version guides the response toward a practical school communication that sounds human and clear. It also keeps the model from wandering off into policy language nobody asked for. A minor miracle.

### Sample prompt 3: Natural version

> Write a friendly Instagram caption for first-time gardeners that explains three easy herbs to grow on a sunny windowsill, and keep it upbeat and simple.

**How the template shows up:**
- **F, Format:** "Instagram caption" tells ChatGPT the output type.
- **A, Audience:** "first-time gardeners" identifies who the content is for.
- **S, Style:** "friendly," "upbeat," and "simple" shape the tone.
- **T, Task:** "explains three easy herbs to grow on a sunny windowsill" gives the specific job.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This version proves you do not always need labels. FAST can be built into a normal sentence. The prompt still gives clear instructions about output, audience, tone, and purpose, so the answer is more likely to fit social media and make sense to beginners. Short, useful, and not trying too hard. A respectable combo.

## Warnings and extra tips

FAST is helpful, but it is not magic. A few things to watch for:

- **Do not confuse style with format.**  
  "Bullet list" is format. "Friendly and persuasive" is style. Mixing them together can make prompts messy.

- **Audience matters more than people think.**  
  If you skip the audience, ChatGPT may aim too high, too low, or too generic. Writing for executives is not the same as writing for fifth graders or customers.

- **Task should be specific.**  
  "Help me with my business" is not a task. "Write a 100-word product description for handmade candles" is a task.

- **FAST may not be enough for complex work.**  
  If the job needs background information, examples, constraints, or source material, add those too. FAST is a strong start, not the entire toolbox.

- **Overstuffing the prompt can backfire.**  
  If you pile on ten style instructions, five audiences, and three formats, the result may get confused. Pick the most important details.

- **Check the result against reality.**  
  FAST improves clarity, but it does not guarantee truth, accuracy, or perfect judgment. If the output involves facts, numbers, medical topics, legal topics, or business decisions, review it carefully.

A good rule: use FAST first, then add extra context if needed.

## Quick summary

FAST stands for **Format, Audience, Style, Task**. It helps you tell ChatGPT:
- what kind of output you want,
- who it is for,
- how it should sound,
- and what job it should do.

That simple structure leads to clearer prompts and more useful answers.

Try FAST on something ordinary today: a work email, a recipe summary, a social post, a study guide, or a message to customers. Pick one task, define the format, audience, and style, then see how much better the response gets. Small prompt upgrade, big difference.
