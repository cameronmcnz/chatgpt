# PAWS

PAWS is a simple way to make your prompts behave. Instead of tossing a vague request at ChatGPT and hoping for the best, you give it four things: a **Persona**, an **Ask**, **Warnings**, and a **Structure**. In other words, you stop prompting like a raccoon in a trash can and start prompting like someone who expects usable results.

PAWS belongs to **Controlled Prompting**, the stage where you stop merely asking and start steering the answer.

## What the template means

| Letter or word | Prompt ingredient | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **P, Persona** | **Role** | Tells ChatGPT what identity or perspective to use, such as teacher, analyst, editor, coach, or marketer. |
| **A, Ask** | **Task** | States what you want done. This is the actual job: write, explain, summarize, compare, brainstorm, rewrite, and so on. |
| **W, Warnings** | **Guardrails** | Tells ChatGPT what to avoid, be careful about, or not include. This reduces fluff, errors, and weird detours. |
| **S, Structure** | **Format** | Specifies how the answer should be organized, such as bullet points, table, step-by-step guide, email, or short paragraph. |

## Why this template works

PAWS works because most bad prompts fail in predictable ways:

- They do not say who the AI should be.
- They do not clearly say what job needs to be done.
- They forget to include boundaries.
- They leave the output format up to chance.

That is how you end up with a five-paragraph motivational speech when you wanted a clean checklist.

PAWS fixes that by giving the model four anchors:

1. **Persona** shapes the voice and expertise.
2. **Ask** defines the job.
3. **Warnings** prevent common mistakes.
4. **Structure** makes the output easier to use.

This makes prompts clearer, more reliable, and easier to reuse. Once you get used to PAWS, you can build prompts faster and edit them with less guesswork.

## 3 sample prompts

### Sample prompt 1: Formal version

> **PERSONA:** Act as a patient financial literacy instructor for adults who are new to budgeting.  
> **ASK:** Explain how to create a simple monthly budget for a household with irregular income.  
> **WARNINGS:** Do not use jargon without defining it. Do not give legal or investment advice. Do not assume the reader has spreadsheet experience.  
> **STRUCTURE:** Provide the answer in 5 numbered steps, followed by a short example budget with plain-language category labels.

**How the template shows up:**
- **P, Persona:** "Act as a patient financial literacy instructor" gives ChatGPT a clear role and teaching style.
- **A, Ask:** "Explain how to create a simple monthly budget for a household with irregular income" defines the exact task.
- **W, Warnings:** The prompt blocks jargon, risky advice, and unrealistic assumptions about the reader.
- **S, Structure:** It asks for 5 numbered steps plus a short example budget.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This version is strong because it leaves very little to chance. The role keeps the explanation approachable. The task is specific, so the answer is less likely to wander into general money philosophy. The warnings reduce confusion and keep the model inside safe, practical boundaries. The structure makes the output useful right away, instead of forcing the user to reorganize a wall of text afterward.

### Sample prompt 2: Semi-structured version

> You are a friendly project manager.  
> Help me turn a messy to-do list into a simple weekly action plan.  
> Keep it realistic, do not overload any single day, and flag anything that sounds unclear or missing.  
> Use a table with columns for task, priority, estimated time, and suggested day.

**How the template shows up:**
- **P, Persona:** "You are a friendly project manager" sets the perspective.
- **A, Ask:** "Help me turn a messy to-do list into a simple weekly action plan" states the job.
- **W, Warnings:** "Keep it realistic, do not overload any single day, and flag anything that sounds unclear or missing" adds guardrails.
- **S, Structure:** "Use a table with columns for task, priority, estimated time, and suggested day" defines the format.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This prompt is less formal, but still controlled. The persona encourages organized thinking. The ask turns a vague wish, "help me with my week," into a concrete transformation. The warnings protect against one of ChatGPT's favorite hobbies, acting wildly confident about missing information. The structure creates an answer you can actually scan and use, not just admire briefly before forgetting like an old office mission statement.

### Sample prompt 3: Natural version

> Pretend you're a calm travel planner and help me figure out a three-day weekend in Chicago. Keep it budget-friendly, skip anything too touristy, and if you're unsure about prices or hours, say so. Lay it out day by day with morning, afternoon, and evening ideas.

**How the template shows up:**
- **P, Persona:** "Pretend you're a calm travel planner" gives the AI a role.
- **A, Ask:** "Help me figure out a three-day weekend in Chicago" is the task.
- **W, Warnings:** "Keep it budget-friendly, skip anything too touristy, and if you're unsure about prices or hours, say so" sets limits and caution.
- **S, Structure:** "Lay it out day by day with morning, afternoon, and evening ideas" defines the output shape.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This one sounds natural, but it still uses PAWS. That is the beauty of the method. You do not always need robotic labels. The role guides the tone, the ask gives purpose, the warnings stop low-quality guesses, and the structure turns a generic travel answer into a practical itinerary. Casual does not have to mean chaotic.

## Warnings and extra tips

PAWS is useful, but it is not magic. A few things to keep in mind:

- **Do not make the Persona overly dramatic.** Asking ChatGPT to act like "the world's greatest genius ninja CEO poet strategist" usually adds noise, not quality.
- **Make the Ask specific enough to complete.** "Help with marketing" is fog. "Write three email subject lines for a spring sale" is something the model can actually do.
- **Warnings should prevent problems, not strangle the answer.** If you add too many restrictions, the model may become stiff, repetitive, or oddly cautious.
- **Structure should fit the task.** A table is great for comparisons, but terrible for a heartfelt apology email.
- **Guardrails do not guarantee truth.** If accuracy matters, ask the model to mark uncertainty, cite sources when possible, or separate facts from guesses.
- **PAWS is not always enough by itself.** Some tasks also need examples, audience details, tone, or hard limits like word count.

A good rule: if the response feels off, check which PAWS element is weak or missing. Usually it is one of those four.

## Quick summary

PAWS helps you control prompts with four simple ingredients:

- **Persona:** Who should ChatGPT be?
- **Ask:** What should it do?
- **Warnings:** What should it avoid or be careful about?
- **Structure:** What shape should the answer take?

If your prompts have ever felt vague, messy, or a little too "good luck, everybody," PAWS gives you a cleaner system.

Try it next with something you already do in real life: planning a meal, writing an email, organizing a trip, or learning a new topic. Take one plain prompt, add PAWS, and compare the results. That is where the light bulb usually turns on.
