# SIM

SIM is a simple prompt recipe for interactive AI: set the **Scenario**, assign an **Identity**, and give a clear **Mission**. That is it. Three moving parts, zero glitter, and a much better chance of getting something useful instead of a weirdly confident mess.

It is especially handy when you want AI to teach, coach, roleplay, simulate a real-life situation, or help you practice before the stakes are real. Think rehearsal mode, not magic mode.

## What the template means

| Letter or word | Prompt ingredient | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| S, Scenario | Scenario | Describes the situation or setting. This gives the AI context for what is happening. |
| I, Identity | Role | Tells the AI who it is supposed to be in that situation, such as a tutor, interviewer, manager, or customer. |
| M, Mission | Goal | Explains what the AI should do in the situation, such as teach, question, guide, simulate, or evaluate. |

## Why this template works

A lot of weak prompts fail for a boring reason: they ask for an answer without setting the scene.

If you only say, “Help me practice for an interview,” the AI has to guess:
- What kind of interview?
- Who is it pretending to be?
- What exactly should happen?

SIM fixes that by forcing three useful decisions:

1. **Scenario** gives context.  
   Context changes the response. A job interview, a sales call, and a parent-teacher meeting all require different language and expectations.

2. **Identity** gives behavior.  
   AI acts differently if it is a hiring manager, a patient tutor, a skeptical client, or a strict examiner.

3. **Mission** gives direction.  
   The AI needs a job to do. Ask it to simulate, explain, test, coach, or provide feedback.

This is why SIM works so well in learning and practice. It turns a vague request into a structured exercise. Instead of “say stuff about spreadsheets,” you get something closer to an actual training moment.

## 3 sample prompts

### Sample prompt 1: Formal version

> SCENARIO: You are conducting a mock job interview for an entry-level project coordinator position at a mid-sized marketing company.  
> IDENTITY: Act as a professional hiring manager who asks realistic interview questions and gives constructive feedback.  
> MISSION: Interview me one question at a time, wait for my answer, then briefly evaluate my response and ask the next question. After 5 questions, give me a summary of my strengths, weak spots, and 3 ways to improve.

**How the template shows up:**
- **S, Scenario:** The prompt defines the exact situation, a mock interview for a specific role at a marketing company.
- **I, Identity:** The AI is assigned the role of a professional hiring manager.
- **M, Mission:** The AI must conduct the interview step by step, evaluate answers, and provide final feedback.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This version is strong because nothing important is left to guessing. The AI knows the setting, the character it should play, and the task flow. That produces a more realistic interview and better feedback. It also avoids the classic prompt problem where the AI writes all five questions at once like it is trying to speedrun your anxiety.

### Sample prompt 2: Semi-structured version

> I need help practicing Spanish for travel.  
>  
> - Situation: I am at a hotel in Madrid trying to check in, ask about breakfast, and find out when checkout is.  
> - Your role: Be the hotel receptionist. Friendly, but only speak simple Spanish unless I ask for help.  
> - Goal: Roleplay the conversation with me step by step. After each of my replies, correct major mistakes in English, then continue the roleplay.

**How the template shows up:**
- **S, Scenario:** The scene is a hotel check-in in Madrid, with specific travel-related tasks.
- **I, Identity:** The AI becomes a hotel receptionist with a defined communication style.
- **M, Mission:** The AI must roleplay the interaction, correct mistakes, and keep the practice going.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This prompt works because it creates a practical language-learning exercise instead of a generic “teach me Spanish” request. The scenario makes the vocabulary relevant. The identity makes the conversation feel believable. The mission adds a learning loop, practice plus correction, which is where actual improvement happens.

### Sample prompt 3: Natural version

> Pretend I am a customer calling because my internet has been down since this morning. Be a calm support agent, ask me troubleshooting questions one at a time, and help me practice answering clearly without sounding angry.

**How the template shows up:**
- **S, Scenario:** The situation is a customer support call about internet service being down.
- **I, Identity:** The AI is told to act as a calm support agent.
- **M, Mission:** The AI should ask troubleshooting questions one by one and help the user practice calm, clear responses.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
Even though this one sounds casual, it still uses SIM well. The setting is clear, the AI has a role, and the goal is specific. That makes the exchange focused and useful. It is also a good reminder that prompt engineering does not always need to look like a government form from 1994.

## Warnings and extra tips

SIM is useful, but do not treat it like a magic spell. A few things can still go sideways.

- **Do not make the scenario too vague.**  
  “Pretend we are in a business situation” is not much of a situation. Give enough detail to shape the response.

- **Do not make the mission too broad.**  
  “Help me with everything” is how you get generic output. Say what success looks like.

- **Identity affects tone.**  
  If you assign the AI a role like “strict examiner” or “skeptical customer,” expect that tone to show up. This is a feature, not a bug, unless you forget you asked for it.

- **SIM is great for interaction, not always enough for formatting.**  
  If you also need a specific output format, length, reading level, or style, add that separately.

- **Realism matters.**  
  If you want practice for a real task, make the scenario resemble real life. A realistic simulation usually teaches more than a vague one.

- **Use follow-up prompts.**  
  After the first round, you can refine the simulation. For example: “Make the interviewer tougher,” “Use simpler Spanish,” or “Give more detailed feedback.”

- **Do not expect perfect expertise in every field.**  
  AI can simulate many roles well, but for legal, medical, financial, or safety-critical training, verify important advice with a qualified human. The AI can rehearse with you, but it is not your licensed professional.

## Quick summary

SIM stands for **Scenario, Identity, Mission**. It helps you create better prompts by answering three questions:

- What is happening?
- Who is the AI?
- What is the AI supposed to do?

That simple structure turns vague requests into useful simulations, practice sessions, and coaching prompts.

Try it next with something real from your life: a job interview, a customer complaint, a presentation rehearsal, a language practice session, or a tough conversation you want to handle better. Pick the scene, assign the role, define the mission, and let the rehearsal begin.
