# PICK

PICK is for those moments when you do not just want ideas, you want a decision. It gives ChatGPT a job that is more useful than "tell me stuff." You ask it to sort options, inspect them, compare them, and then actually keep the best one. Revolutionary, I know.

This template fits the **Analysis and Decision-Making** stage, where ChatGPT becomes less like a search box and more like a thinking partner.

## What the template means

| Letter | Prompt ingredient | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **P** | **Prioritize** | Tells ChatGPT to rank or sort the options by importance, value, or fit. |
| **I** | **Inspect** | Tells ChatGPT to examine each option carefully, not just react with a shallow opinion. |
| **C** | **Compare** | Tells ChatGPT to weigh options against each other using useful criteria. |
| **K** | **Keep** | Tells ChatGPT to choose the strongest option and explain why it wins. |

## Why this template works

A lot of weak prompts ask for analysis but never say what kind. The result is vague summaries, random pros and cons, or the classic AI move: "It depends."

PICK fixes that.

It gives ChatGPT a clear decision process:

1. **Prioritize** what matters.
2. **Inspect** each option.
3. **Compare** them directly.
4. **Keep** the best one.

That structure matters because decision-making is not just listing facts. It is narrowing choices. If your prompt skips the ranking, the criteria, or the final choice, ChatGPT may hedge forever like a manager trying not to approve a budget.

PICK is especially helpful when you need:
- a recommendation between tools, products, or plans,
- help choosing between competing ideas,
- a clearer final answer instead of a mushy overview,
- reasoning you can actually use in real life.

## 3 sample prompts

### Sample prompt 1: Formal version

> PRIORITIZE: Rank these three project management tools for a 10-person remote marketing team: Trello, Asana, and Monday.com. Prioritize ease of onboarding, collaboration, reporting, and cost.  
> INSPECT: Evaluate each tool on those criteria and note any important limitations or assumptions.  
> COMPARE: Compare the three tools in a table first, then in a short written analysis.  
> KEEP: Recommend the single best choice for this team and explain why it should be kept over the others.

**How the template shows up:**
- **P, Prioritize:** The prompt tells ChatGPT exactly what matters most: onboarding, collaboration, reporting, and cost.
- **I, Inspect:** It asks for evaluation of each tool and requests limitations and assumptions.
- **C, Compare:** It explicitly asks for a comparison table and a written comparison.
- **K, Keep:** It requires one final recommendation, not a shrug and a "depends."

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This prompt is strong because it gives a decision context, clear criteria, and a required final choice. Without PICK, ChatGPT might produce a generic product summary. With PICK, it follows a useful decision path and produces something a real team could act on.

### Sample prompt 2: Semi-structured version

> I am choosing a learning path for someone new to data analysis.  
> Options: Excel, SQL, or Python.  
>   
> Please:  
> - prioritize the best starting point for getting job-ready quickly,  
> - inspect each option for learning curve, usefulness, and hiring value,  
> - compare them clearly,  
> - keep one as the best first skill to learn, with a brief reason.

**How the template shows up:**
- **P, Prioritize:** "Best starting point for getting job-ready quickly" creates a ranking goal.
- **I, Inspect:** The prompt asks ChatGPT to inspect learning curve, usefulness, and hiring value.
- **C, Compare:** It directly requests a clear comparison among Excel, SQL, and Python.
- **K, Keep:** It asks ChatGPT to keep one option as the best first skill.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This version feels more natural but still keeps the decision structure. It prevents ChatGPT from treating all three skills as equally good, which is often technically safe and practically useless. PICK pushes the model toward a practical recommendation tied to a real goal: getting job-ready quickly.

### Sample prompt 3: Natural version

> I am trying to pick the best way to spend my Saturday mornings for the next three months: going to the gym, taking an online course, or doing freelance work. Look at which option would help me most with long-term career growth, personal health, and extra income, then tell me which one you think I should stick with.

**How the template shows up:**
- **P, Prioritize:** The prompt names the decision factors: career growth, personal health, and extra income.
- **I, Inspect:** "Look at which option would help me most" tells ChatGPT to examine each choice against those factors.
- **C, Compare:** The three options are set side by side for direct comparison.
- **K, Keep:** "Tell me which one you think I should stick with" asks for a final pick.

**Why it improves the prompt:**
This casual version proves you do not need robotic formatting to use PICK. The structure is still there. The prompt works because it includes options, criteria, and a final decision request. That makes the answer more focused and more actionable than a vague "what should I do with my time?" prompt.

## Warnings and extra tips

PICK is useful, but it is not magic. A few things to watch out for:

- **Bad options lead to bad decisions.** If all the choices are weak, ChatGPT can still only choose from the menu you gave it.
- **Criteria matter more than confidence.** If you do not define what "best" means, the answer may sound polished but rest on hidden assumptions.
- **Too many options can muddy the result.** PICK works best when the list is manageable. Three to five options is usually better than fifteen.
- **ChatGPT may assume facts.** Ask it to note assumptions, especially for costs, timelines, or industry-specific claims.
- **Sometimes PICK is not enough.** If you need risks, objections, or hidden trade-offs, you may need to add those directly. Decision-making is messy. Sadly, there is no template that makes reality behave.
- **Use real context.** Include your budget, goals, time frame, skill level, or team size. The more grounded the prompt, the less generic the answer.

A good upgrade is to add one extra line like:  
**"If the best choice depends on assumptions, state those assumptions before choosing."**

That keeps the recommendation honest.

## Quick summary

PICK helps you turn ChatGPT into a decision assistant:

- **Prioritize** what matters
- **Inspect** each option
- **Compare** them clearly
- **Keep** the best one

If your prompt needs a recommendation, not just a brainstorm, PICK is a solid move.

Try it next with a real choice from your life: software, courses, job options, meal plans, side hustles, or even which streaming service deserves your money this month. Make ChatGPT earn its keep.
