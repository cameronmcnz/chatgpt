# PAWS: Persona, Ask, Warnings, Structure

If your prompts feel random, your answers will too. ChatGPT is not a mind reader, it is more like a very fast intern with zero context and a dangerous amount of confidence.

That is where **PAWS** helps. It gives you a simple way to ask for better results without writing a giant novel every time.

## Why this matters

Beginners often type something like, “Write me an email,” and then wonder why the result sounds stiff, vague, or like it was written by a robot wearing a blazer.

The problem is not always ChatGPT. Often, the prompt is missing key ingredients. PAWS fixes that by helping you include four things that shape a better answer:

- **Persona**: who ChatGPT should be
- **Ask**: what you want it to do
- **Warnings**: what to avoid
- **Structure**: how the answer should be organized

It is a simple framework, and simple is good. Not every prompt needs to look like a NASA launch checklist.

## The idea in plain English

Here is PAWS broken down:

| Part | What it means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Persona** | Give ChatGPT a role or viewpoint | “You are a friendly career coach” |
| **Ask** | State the task clearly | “Help me write a follow-up email after an interview” |
| **Warnings** | Say what to avoid | “Do not sound pushy or overly formal” |
| **Structure** | Tell it how to format the answer | “Give me 3 versions: short, warm, and polished” |

PAWS works because it removes guessing.

Instead of this:

“Write me an email.”

You get this:

“You are a friendly career coach. Help me write a follow-up email after a job interview. Do not sound desperate, stiff, or fake. Give me 3 versions: short, warm, and polished.”

Much better. Same task, better instructions.

## Simple example

Let’s say you want help with a meal plan.

**Weak prompt:**

“Make me a meal plan.”

**PAWS version:**

“You are a practical home cook helping a busy adult. Create a 5-day dinner meal plan. Avoid expensive ingredients and complicated recipes. Structure it as a simple list with each meal, prep time, and a short shopping list.”

That prompt tells ChatGPT:
- what role to play
- what to do
- what not to do
- what the output should look like

That is the difference between getting something useful and getting culinary fan fiction.

## Quick tips

- **Keep the persona realistic**. “Helpful math tutor” is better than “galactic wisdom master.”
- **Make the ask specific**. Clear tasks get clearer answers.
- **Use warnings to prevent common mistakes**. Think tone, length, jargon, or bad assumptions.
- **Add structure when format matters**. Bullets, table, steps, email draft, checklist, and so on.
- **Do not overbuild every prompt**. PAWS is a guide, not a legal contract.

## Quick summary

**PAWS** stands for **Persona, Ask, Warnings, Structure**. It helps you write prompts that are clear, focused, and easier for ChatGPT to answer well. If your results feel off, PAWS is a quick way to tighten the prompt and get something more useful on the first try.
