# Using an LLM as a Tutor

If you have ever read an explanation from ChatGPT and thought, “Great, I recognize all these words, and somehow understand none of them,” this lesson is for you.

One of the most useful ways to use ChatGPT is not as a magic answer machine, but as a tutor. A good tutor does not just dump information on your head like a phone book falling off a shelf. It teaches at your level, checks understanding, and adjusts as you go.

## Why this matters

Beginners often ask AI a question, get a polished answer, and then hit a wall. The answer looks smart, but it is too fast, too broad, or too technical.

Using an LLM as a tutor solves that problem. You can ask it to:
- explain things in plain English
- teach step by step
- quiz you
- give practice questions
- correct your misunderstandings
- slow down when your brain starts playing dial-up internet sounds

This turns ChatGPT from “instant answer machine” into “patient, available anytime learning partner.”

## The idea in plain English

When you use ChatGPT as a tutor, you are telling it *how* to teach, not just *what* to answer.

That means you can ask it to:
- teach like a beginner-friendly instructor
- avoid jargon unless it explains the jargon
- give one step at a time
- ask you questions before moving on
- use examples from real life
- check your work and explain mistakes

The key is to be specific about your level and the teaching style you want.

## Simple example

Instead of this:

**Prompt:**  
Explain compound interest.

Try this:

**Prompt:**  
Act as a patient tutor. Teach me compound interest like I am brand new to it. Use simple language, one step at a time. Start with a short explanation, then give me a real-world example, then ask me one question to check my understanding before continuing.

That prompt changes the whole experience. Now the LLM is not just answering, it is teaching.

Here is a handy comparison:

| Weak prompt | Better tutor prompt |
|---|---|
| Explain photosynthesis. | Teach me photosynthesis like a beginner. Use simple language, avoid jargon, and explain it in 3 short steps. Then quiz me with 2 questions. |
| Help me learn Excel formulas. | Act as an Excel tutor. I know the basics but get confused by formulas. Teach me SUM, AVERAGE, and IF with tiny examples and short practice exercises. |
| What is the stock market? | Teach me what the stock market is using plain English and a simple analogy. Then tell me 3 common beginner misunderstandings. |

## Quick tips

- Tell it your level: beginner, rusty, intermediate, or advanced.
- Ask for one step at a time if you get overwhelmed easily.
- Ask it to quiz you instead of only explaining.
- Ask it to use analogies, examples, or practice exercises.
- If the explanation is too fancy, say: “Make that simpler.”
- If it moves too fast, say: “Teach this like a tutor, not like a textbook.”

## Quick summary

Using an LLM as a tutor means asking it to teach in a way that fits you. Instead of only requesting answers, ask for explanations, examples, check-ins, and practice. Done well, ChatGPT becomes less like a search result and more like a calm, infinitely patient study partner.
