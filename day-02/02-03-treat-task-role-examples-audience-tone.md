# TREAT: Task, Role, Examples, Audience, Tone

A vague prompt gets you a vague answer. Shocking, I know. If you’ve ever typed something like “write me an email” and then stared at the reply thinking, “Well, that’s technically English,” TREAT fixes that.

## Why this matters

Beginners often assume ChatGPT can read their mind. It cannot. It is good with patterns, not telepathy.

The TREAT framework gives your prompt five simple ingredients so the response is more useful on the first try:

- **Task**: What you want done
- **Role**: Who ChatGPT should act like
- **Examples**: A sample of what good looks like
- **Audience**: Who the answer is for
- **Tone**: How it should sound

This helps you stop getting bland, generic responses that sound like they were approved by a committee in 1997.

## The idea in plain English

Think of TREAT like giving directions to a helpful assistant.

If you only say, “Make dinner,” you might get toast.
If you say, “Make a quick pasta dinner, like an Italian home cook, similar to this recipe style, for two tired adults, in a casual tone,” now we’re getting somewhere.

Here’s the framework in a simple table:

| Part | What it means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Task** | The job to do | “Write a reminder email” |
| **Role** | The perspective or expertise | “Act as a polite office manager” |
| **Examples** | A sample to imitate | “Use this short email as a model” |
| **Audience** | Who it’s meant for | “For busy clients” |
| **Tone** | The style of writing | “Friendly, clear, not pushy” |

You do not need all five every single time. But when a response feels off, one of these is usually missing.

## Simple example

Let’s say you want help writing a message.

**Weak prompt:**

“Write a message about our meeting.”

That leaves way too much open.

**TREAT version:**

“Write a short meeting reminder email. Act as a professional but friendly team coordinator. Use this style as an example: brief, clear, and warm. The audience is busy department managers. Keep the tone polite and direct.”

That version gives ChatGPT a job, a voice, a model, a reader, and a style. Much better.

## Quick tips

- Start with **Task** first. If the job is fuzzy, the answer will be too.
- Use **Role** to shape expertise, not to play dress-up for no reason.
- Add **Examples** when you want a specific format or style.
- Always think about **Audience**. A message for your boss should not sound like a text to your cousin.
- Be specific with **Tone**. “Professional” is okay, but “professional, warm, concise” is better.
- If the first answer misses the mark, adjust one TREAT element instead of rewriting everything.

## Quick summary

TREAT is a simple way to write better prompts: tell ChatGPT the **Task**, give it a **Role**, provide **Examples**, name the **Audience**, and set the **Tone**. When you do that, your prompts get clearer, and the replies sound a lot less random.
