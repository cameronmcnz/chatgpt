# CRAP: Context, Restrictions, Aim, Persona

A lot of bad ChatGPT answers are not because the AI is "dumb." They happen because the prompt is basically, "Read my mind." That is not a strategy. That is a hostage negotiation.

CRAP is a simple prompt framework that helps you ask more clearly. The name is a little rude, yes, but it works because it is memorable: **Context, Restrictions, Aim, Persona**.

## Why this matters

Beginners often type something short like, "Write me an email" or "Help me with this project." ChatGPT will respond, but it has to guess what you mean.

That guesswork causes common problems:
- The tone is off
- The answer is too long or too vague
- It solves the wrong problem
- It sounds like it was written for someone else entirely

CRAP fixes that by giving ChatGPT the information it needs upfront.

## The idea in plain English

Here is what each part means:

| Part | What it means | Simple question to ask yourself |
|---|---|---|
| **Context** | Background information | What is going on here? |
| **Restrictions** | Limits or rules | What should it avoid or stay within? |
| **Aim** | The goal | What do I want this to achieve? |
| **Persona** | The role or voice | Who should ChatGPT act like? |

Think of it like giving directions to a new assistant. If you only say, "Handle this," good luck. If you explain the situation, the rules, the goal, and the role, you usually get something useful.

## Simple example

Weak prompt:

> Write a message to my team about a schedule change.

Better prompt using CRAP:

> **Context:** I manage a small customer support team.  
> **Restrictions:** Keep it under 120 words, clear and friendly, no corporate jargon.  
> **Aim:** Inform the team that next week's Monday meeting is moving from 9 AM to 11 AM, and ask them to confirm they saw the change.  
> **Persona:** Act like a calm, professional team lead.

See the difference? Now ChatGPT knows the situation, the limits, the goal, and the voice. Much less guessing, much better result.

## Quick tips

- **Start with Aim if you are in a hurry.** If the goal is unclear, everything else falls apart.
- **Use Restrictions to control quality.** Add word count, tone, reading level, format, or things to avoid.
- **Persona is not just for fun.** It can make ChatGPT sound like a teacher, editor, coach, recruiter, or project manager.
- **Do not overstuff Context.** Include what matters, not your entire life story.
- **If the answer is off, check which CRAP part is missing.** Usually it is context or restrictions.

## Quick summary

CRAP stands for **Context, Restrictions, Aim, Persona**. It is a simple way to write better prompts by telling ChatGPT what is happening, what rules to follow, what outcome you want, and what role it should take. When your prompt includes those four pieces, the response usually gets a lot more useful, and a lot less "well, that was not what I meant."
