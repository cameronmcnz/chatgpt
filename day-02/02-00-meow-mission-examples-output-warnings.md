# MEOW: A Simple Prompt Formula That Actually Works

If your prompts feel like tossing a note into the void and hoping for the best, MEOW fixes that. It gives ChatGPT four simple things to work with, so you stop getting vague, weird, or almost-helpful answers.

## Why this matters

Beginners often type something like, “Write me an email,” and then wonder why the result sounds stiff, too long, or oddly dramatic, like a soap opera audition.

The problem usually is not ChatGPT. It is that the request is missing key pieces. MEOW helps you include those pieces without turning every prompt into a legal contract.

MEOW stands for:

- **Mission**
- **Examples**
- **Output**
- **Warnings**

Think of it as giving directions that a reasonably smart intern can actually follow.

## The idea in plain English

### Mission
This is the job you want done.

What do you want ChatGPT to create, explain, rewrite, brainstorm, or summarize?

Example:
- “Write a friendly follow-up email to a client.”
- “Summarize this article in plain English.”
- “Brainstorm 10 blog post ideas for a dog grooming business.”

### Examples
Show what “good” looks like.

Examples are incredibly helpful because they reduce guessing. If you give ChatGPT a sample style, format, or type of response, the output usually improves fast.

Example:
- “Here is a sample of my writing style.”
- “Make it similar to these subject lines.”
- “Use this format: greeting, update, next steps, sign-off.”

### Output
Tell it exactly how the answer should appear.

This is where you specify format, length, structure, tone, or reading level.

Example:
- “Keep it under 120 words.”
- “Use bullet points.”
- “Write at a 6th grade reading level.”
- “Give me a table with pros and cons.”

### Warnings
These are the guardrails.

What should ChatGPT avoid? What would make the answer useless, awkward, or inaccurate?

Example:
- “Do not sound pushy.”
- “Avoid jargon.”
- “Do not make up statistics.”
- “Do not use emojis.”

## Simple example

Here is a basic weak prompt:

> Write a follow-up email after a meeting.

Now the MEOW version:

| Part | Prompt content |
|---|---|
| **Mission** | Write a follow-up email after a short meeting with a potential client. |
| **Examples** | Use a warm, professional style like this: “Thanks for your time today. I enjoyed learning more about your goals.” |
| **Output** | Keep it under 150 words. Include a thank you, one recap sentence, and a clear next step. |
| **Warnings** | Do not sound salesy. Do not overpromise. Do not use corporate buzzwords. |

That version gives ChatGPT a much better shot at getting it right the first time.

## Quick tips

- Start with **Mission** and **Output** if you want to keep it simple.
- Add **Examples** when style really matters.
- Add **Warnings** when there are things you definitely do not want.
- If the result is close but not right, tweak one part instead of rewriting everything.
- MEOW is a framework, not a sacred artifact. Use what helps, skip what does not.

## Quick summary

MEOW helps you write clearer prompts by answering four questions: What do you want, what should it resemble, what form should it take, and what should it avoid? If your prompts have been a little chaotic, MEOW brings order without making prompt writing feel like programming.
