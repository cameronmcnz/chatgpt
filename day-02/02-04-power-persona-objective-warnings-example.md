# POWER Prompting: Persona, Objective, Warnings, Example

If your prompts feel like tossing vague instructions into the void and hoping for magic, POWER helps fix that fast.

POWER is a simple prompt framework: **Persona, Objective, Warnings, Example**. It gives ChatGPT a role, a goal, a few guardrails, and a model to follow. Think of it like giving directions to someone before they leave the driveway, instead of yelling “just head that way” and hoping for the best.

## Why this matters

Beginners often ask for something broad, like “write an email” or “help me plan dinner,” then get a response that is technically fine, but not actually useful.

That happens because ChatGPT is filling in the blanks on its own. Sometimes it guesses well. Sometimes it guesses like a 1997 GPS with a scratched CD.

POWER reduces the guessing.

## The idea in plain English

Here’s what each part does:

| Part | What it means | Why it helps |
|---|---|---|
| **Persona** | Who ChatGPT should act as | Sets the style or perspective |
| **Objective** | What you want it to do | Keeps the answer focused |
| **Warnings** | What to avoid or be careful about | Prevents unhelpful output |
| **Example** | A sample of the kind of result you want | Shows the format, tone, or structure |

You do not need a giant prompt. You just need these four parts to be clear.

A simple POWER prompt looks like this:

- **Persona:** Act as a practical career coach  
- **Objective:** Help me write a short follow-up email after a job interview  
- **Warnings:** Keep it professional, not stiff. Do not sound desperate. Keep it under 120 words  
- **Example:** Similar tone to: “Thank you for your time today. I enjoyed learning more about the role and your team.”

That gives ChatGPT much better odds of producing something you can actually use.

## Simple example

Here’s a full prompt:

**Persona:** Act as an experienced home cooking teacher.  
**Objective:** Create a simple 3-night dinner plan for a busy week.  
**Warnings:** Use easy ingredients, keep each meal under 30 minutes, avoid fancy techniques, and do not include seafood.  
**Example:** One meal could be “sheet pan chicken, potatoes, and broccoli.”

That prompt is much stronger than just saying:  
“Give me dinner ideas.”

Why? Because now ChatGPT knows the role, the goal, the limits, and the style of answer you want.

## Quick tips

- Start with the **objective** if you are in a hurry. Add the rest as needed.
- Use **warnings** to prevent common annoyances, like “do not be too wordy” or “avoid jargon.”
- Your **example** can be tiny. Even one sentence helps.
- Pick a **persona** that improves the result, not one that sounds clever.
- If the first answer is off, keep the same POWER structure and revise one part.

## Quick summary

POWER stands for **Persona, Objective, Warnings, Example**. It helps ChatGPT give better answers by making your prompt clearer and more specific. Give it a role, tell it the goal, add a few guardrails, and show a small example. Less guessing, better results.
