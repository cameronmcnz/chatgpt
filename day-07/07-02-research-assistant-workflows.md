# Research Assistant Workflows with ChatGPT

Ever spent 45 minutes researching something, only to end up with 17 tabs open, three conflicting answers, and the vague feeling that you now know less than when you started? That is exactly where a research assistant workflow helps.

## Why this matters

Most beginners use ChatGPT for research in a very loose way. They ask one big question, get one big answer, and hope for the best. The problem is that research usually needs structure, not one magical prompt.

A good research workflow turns ChatGPT into a helper for gathering, organizing, comparing, and summarizing information. It does not replace your judgment. It saves time, reduces chaos, and helps you ask better follow-up questions.

Think of it less like a crystal ball, more like a very fast intern who needs supervision.

## The idea in plain English

A research assistant workflow is a repeatable sequence of steps you use when exploring a topic.

Instead of asking:

> "Tell me everything about electric cars."

You break the job into stages, like this:

1. Define the goal.
2. Ask for a research plan.
3. Break the topic into subtopics.
4. Compare options, viewpoints, or sources.
5. Summarize findings in a useful format.
6. Identify gaps or questions to verify elsewhere.

This works well for everyday tasks like:
- comparing software tools
- learning a new topic
- planning a purchase
- researching travel options
- gathering background for a meeting, article, or presentation

## Simple example

Let’s say you want to research project management tools for a small team.

You could use a workflow like this:

| Step | Prompt |
|---|---|
| Define the task | "I need to research project management tools for a 5-person team. Our priorities are ease of use, cost, and calendar integration." |
| Break it down | "List the main criteria I should compare before choosing a tool." |
| Compare options | "Compare Trello, Asana, Monday.com, and ClickUp for a small non-technical team." |
| Find tradeoffs | "What are the biggest pros, cons, and hidden gotchas for each?" |
| Summarize | "Create a simple recommendation table with best choice by budget, simplicity, and features." |
| Pressure test | "What questions should I still verify on each company’s website before deciding?" |

That last step matters. ChatGPT is helpful, but it can be confidently wrong, like a guy at RadioShack in 1997 explaining why you definitely need six adapter cables.

## Quick tips

- **Start with the goal**, not just the topic. Say what decision you are trying to make.
- **Ask for categories first** before asking for details.
- **Use comparison prompts** when choosing between options.
- **Ask for missing information**: "What am I not thinking to ask?"
- **Request a final format** you can actually use, like a table, checklist, or recommendation summary.
- **Verify important facts** with trusted sources, especially prices, policies, medical info, legal info, or anything time-sensitive.

## Quick summary

A research assistant workflow gives ChatGPT a job with steps, not just a vague question. Start with your goal, break the topic into parts, compare what matters, summarize the findings, and verify key facts. Done right, it helps you research faster, think more clearly, and keep the tab explosion under control.
