# Custom Instructions: Make ChatGPT Stop Starting From Scratch

If you keep typing the same preferences into every new chat, you're doing extra work for no reason. Custom instructions are how you give ChatGPT your standing directions once, so it stops acting like this is your first meeting every single time.

## Why this matters

Beginners often run into the same annoyance: every new chat feels like a reset. You want concise answers, plain English, a certain tone, maybe examples, maybe bullet points, maybe help aimed at your job or goals. But unless you say that again and again, ChatGPT has no reason to know.

Custom instructions solve that. They let you set your default preferences so responses start closer to what you actually want.

## The idea in plain English

Custom instructions are your built-in preferences for future chats.

Think of them as the sticky note attached to your account that says:

- "Explain things simply"
- "Use bullet points when helpful"
- "Keep the tone professional but friendly"
- "Assume I want practical advice, not fluff"
- "Write for customers, coworkers, or students"
- "Ask a clarifying question if my request is vague"

That does **not** mean ChatGPT will obey perfectly every time. It just means it starts with your preferences in mind.

A good way to think about it: prompts are for the current task, custom instructions are for your usual style.

## Simple example

Let’s say you regularly use ChatGPT for writing emails and learning new tools.

Your custom instructions might include:

| What to include | Example |
|---|---|
| Tone | Friendly, clear, not overly casual |
| Format | Use short paragraphs and bullet points |
| Audience | Assume I often write for clients and coworkers |
| Preference | Explain jargon in plain English |
| Behavior | If a request is unclear, ask 1 to 2 questions first |

Then instead of typing all that every time, you can just say:

**"Help me write a follow-up email after a meeting."**

And ChatGPT is more likely to respond in the style you already asked for.

## Quick tips

- Keep instructions **specific**, not novel-length.
- Focus on **how you like responses delivered**.
- Include your common audiences, like clients, managers, students, or customers.
- Add useful defaults, like "be concise" or "give examples."
- Review them occasionally. Preferences change, and old instructions can become the AI equivalent of a fax machine sitting in the corner, still technically there, still not helping.

## Quick summary

Custom instructions are your default settings for how ChatGPT should respond across new chats. They save time, reduce repetition, and make answers feel more useful right from the start. Use them for your usual tone, format, audience, and preferences, then use regular prompts for the specific task in front of you.
