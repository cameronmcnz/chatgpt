# About Me: The Personalization Setting That Saves You Repeating Yourself

If you keep telling ChatGPT, “I’m a beginner,” “keep it simple,” or “use a friendly tone,” you are basically making it re-read your résumé every time you walk into the room. The **About Me** or **personalization** setting helps fix that.

## Why this matters

Beginners often run into the same annoying problem: ChatGPT gives answers that are too technical, too wordy, too formal, or aimed at the wrong kind of audience.

That usually happens because it does not automatically know much about you unless you tell it. The **About Me** setting gives ChatGPT some useful background so its replies can fit you better from the start.

Think of it like setting the presets on your car radio. You do it once, then stop fiddling every single time.

## The idea in plain English

The **About Me** section is where you share basic information about yourself that can improve future responses.

This might include things like:

- Your experience level
- Your goals
- Your preferred tone
- The type of work you do
- The audience you usually write for
- How detailed or simple you want answers to be

For example, you might tell ChatGPT:

- “I’m new to AI tools.”
- “Explain things in plain English.”
- “I write emails for clients and want a professional but friendly tone.”
- “Keep answers practical and avoid jargon unless you explain it.”

This is not the same as writing a full prompt every time. It is more like giving ChatGPT a standing note about how to help you.

## Simple example

Here’s a basic before-and-after:

| Situation | Result |
|---|---|
| No personalization | You ask a question and get a generic answer that may be too broad or too technical |
| With About Me settings | You ask the same question and get an answer shaped to your skill level, goals, and tone preferences |

Example:

**Without About Me:**  
“Explain prompt engineering.”

You might get a textbook-style answer.

**With About Me saying you are a beginner who likes practical examples:**  
“Explain prompt engineering.”

Now you are more likely to get a simpler explanation, clearer steps, and a real-world example instead of a lecture that sounds like it was written for a conference badge holder.

## Quick tips

- Keep it **short and useful**, not your life story.
- Include things that affect answers often, like skill level, tone, and goals.
- Mention your usual audience if you create content for other people.
- Update it when your needs change.
- Do not put sensitive personal, financial, medical, or private business information in there.

A simple formula works well:

**Who you are + what you do + what kind of help you want**

Example:
“I’m a small business owner with beginner-level AI experience. I want clear, practical help with writing, marketing, and planning. Use a friendly, professional tone and avoid heavy jargon.”

## Quick summary

The **About Me** personalization setting helps ChatGPT tailor answers to you without making you repeat yourself in every chat. Add your experience level, goals, tone preferences, and typical audience. Keep it useful, keep it current, and keep private details out of it.
