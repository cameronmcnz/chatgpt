# Day 1: Getting Started with ChatGPT

Welcome to Day 1. This is where ChatGPT stops being “that AI thing everyone keeps talking about” and starts becoming a practical tool you can actually use.

If you have ever opened ChatGPT, stared at the blank message box, and thought, “So... what exactly am I supposed to type here?” you are in the right place. Today is about getting comfortable, learning the basic language, and understanding when ChatGPT is the right tool for the job.

No computer science degree required. No need to pretend you know what “tokens” are yet. We will get there.

## What this day is about

Today is your friendly starting point for using ChatGPT with confidence.

You will learn what ChatGPT is, how it works at a basic level, and how it compares to tools you may already know, like Google Search. You will also get a practical overview of other major AI tools, including Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and Microsoft Copilot.

The goal is not to turn you into an AI engineer by lunchtime. The goal is to help you understand what these tools are good at, where they can go wrong, and how to begin using them for everyday tasks.

By the end of the day, ChatGPT should feel less like a mysterious robot in a box and more like a helpful assistant that still needs clear instructions, occasional supervision, and absolutely should not be trusted with your bank password.

## Why this matters

A lot of people try ChatGPT once, get a strange or generic answer, and decide it is overhyped. Usually, the problem is not that the tool is useless. The problem is that the conversation started too vaguely.

ChatGPT works best when you know how to ask clearly, give context, and follow up. It is not just a search engine. It is not a mind reader. It is not a magic eight ball wearing a business suit.

This day matters because it gives you the foundation for everything else in the course. Before you can write better prompts, automate tasks, summarize documents, brainstorm ideas, or use AI at work, you need to understand the basics:

- What ChatGPT can do well
- What it should not be used for
- How it differs from Google and other AI tools
- How to start a useful conversation
- How to fix an answer that misses the mark
- How to stay safe with private or sensitive information

Once you understand these basics, using AI becomes much less intimidating. It also becomes far more useful.

## What you will learn

Today’s learning objectives are practical and beginner-friendly. You will learn how to:

- Explain what ChatGPT is in plain English, without sounding like you swallowed a tech brochure.
- Understand the basic idea behind an LLM, or large language model.
- Describe how ChatGPT is different from Google Search.
- Recognize how ChatGPT compares with Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and Microsoft Copilot.
- Start a conversation with a clear beginner prompt.
- Improve an answer by asking follow-up questions, requesting clarification, or changing the format.
- Use simple prompts when simple is enough, and more detailed prompts when the task needs more direction.
- Recognize common risks, including made-up information, outdated answers, and privacy mistakes.

Most importantly, you will learn that your first prompt does not have to be perfect. ChatGPT is conversational. You can ask it to try again, explain itself, simplify the answer, make a list, change the tone, or focus on a different angle.

That back-and-forth is where the real usefulness begins.

## What we will cover

We will begin with the basics: what ChatGPT is and what people mean when they say “LLM.” You do not need to memorize technical definitions. You just need enough understanding to use the tool wisely and spot its limits.

Then we will compare ChatGPT with Google Search. This is one of the biggest early questions people have, and for good reason. Google is great when you want to find existing information, websites, sources, directions, reviews, or current facts. ChatGPT is often better when you want help explaining, drafting, organizing, summarizing, brainstorming, or turning messy thoughts into something usable.

We will also look at how ChatGPT compares with Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and Microsoft Copilot. Each tool has strengths. Some are better for research-style answers. Some connect more closely with workplace software. Some are better at writing or analyzing longer documents. You do not need to use all of them, but it helps to know they exist and when another tool might be a better fit.

From there, we will cover the basic vocabulary of prompting: prompts, responses, context, tokens, hallucinations, and models. These terms may sound technical, but they are easier than they look. Think of this as learning the dashboard before driving the car.

You will also learn how to start a conversation, ask follow-up questions, regenerate an answer, refine a response, and redirect ChatGPT when it gives you something that is technically an answer but not actually helpful. Yes, that happens. AI can be very confident while being very wrong, which is impressive in the same way a bad GPS route is impressive.

Finally, we will cover basic safety and privacy. You will learn why you should not paste sensitive personal, financial, medical, legal, or confidential work information into ChatGPT. Helpful tool, yes. Secret vault, no.

## Where this becomes useful

The skills from Day 1 show up almost everywhere.

You might use ChatGPT to:

- Draft a professional email without spending 20 minutes fighting with the first sentence.
- Summarize a long article, meeting note, or policy document.
- Brainstorm gift ideas, meal plans, project names, or vacation options.
- Create a checklist before starting a task.
- Rewrite a message so it sounds clearer, warmer, firmer, or more concise.
- Ask for an explanation of a confusing topic in plain language.
- Compare options before making a decision.
- Turn scattered notes into an organized outline.

You will also know when not to use ChatGPT. If you need live news, verified facts, citations, local business hours, current pricing, or highly sensitive advice, another tool or a trusted expert may be better.

That judgment is part of becoming good with AI.

## Quick summary

Day 1 is about getting comfortable, practical, and realistic.

You will learn what ChatGPT is, how it compares to other tools, how to begin a useful conversation, and how to improve answers with follow-up questions. You will also learn the basic safety habits that keep you from treating AI like a diary, filing cabinet, doctor, lawyer, and search engine all at once.

By the end of today, you will not know everything about ChatGPT. Good. Nobody needs that on Day 1.

But you will know enough to start using it on purpose, with more confidence and fewer “what now?” moments. That is the foundation we will build on for the rest of the course.
